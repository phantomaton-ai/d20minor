# d20minor

Welcome to d20minor, a simple roleplaying game of fantasy adventure! This game is designed to be easy to learn and fun to play. You and your friends will create heroic characters who explore a world of magic, monsters, and treasure.

One player will be the **Gamemaster (GM)**. The GM is the storyteller, the referee, and the person who plays the monsters. The other players are the **adventurers**.

## The Core Rule

The most important rule in d20minor is this: when you want to do something, and you don't know if you'll succeed, you roll a **20-sided die (d20)**.

*   **Roll a d20.**
*   **Add a number called a "modifier" to the roll.** This modifier comes from one of your character's abilities.
*   **Compare the total to a "Difficulty Class" (DC)** number set by the GM.

If your total is equal to or higher than the DC, you succeed! If it's lower, you fail.

## The Six Abilities

Every character has six abilities, which describe their strengths and weaknesses.

*   **Strength (STR):** How strong you are. Used for hitting things with heavy weapons, breaking down doors, and lifting heavy things.
*   **Dexterity (DEX):** How quick and agile you are. Used for dodging, sneaking, and using ranged weapons like bows.
*   **Constitution (CON):** How tough you are. Used for taking damage and resisting sickness.
*   **Intelligence (INT):** How smart you are. Used for solving puzzles, remembering information, and casting spells.
*   **Wisdom (WIS):** How aware and perceptive you are. Used for noticing things, tracking, and resisting magic.
*   **Charisma (CHA):** How charming and persuasive you are. Used for talking to people, making friends, and being a leader.

Each ability has a **modifier**, a number that you add to your d20 rolls. A modifier can be negative, zero, or positive. For example, if you have a Strength modifier of +2, you are pretty strong!

## Ability Checks

Sometimes, you'll want to do something that requires a special talent, like picking a lock, climbing a sheer wall, or convincing a guard to let you pass. Other times, you'll need to resist something bad, like a trap, a poison, or a magical spell. In these situations, you'll make an **Ability Check**.

Making an Ability Check is simple. Just like the core rule, you:

*   **Roll a d20.**
*   **Add the modifier for the ability that makes the most sense.** For example, climbing a wall would be a Strength check, while resisting a poison would be a Constitution check. The GM will tell you which ability to use.
*   **Compare the total to a Difficulty Class (DC).**

If your total is equal to or higher than the DC, you succeed!

### Attacking

Attacking a creature is a special kind of Ability Check.

*   **For melee attacks** (with swords, axes, etc.), you make a **Strength check**.
*   **For ranged attacks** (with bows, thrown daggers, etc.), you make a **Dexterity check**.

Instead of comparing your roll to a DC, you compare it to the creature's **Armor Class (AC)**. If you hit, you deal damage!

## Combat

When diplomacy fails, it's time for combat! Here's how to handle a fight in d20minor.

### Initiative

At the start of a fight, everyone rolls a Dexterity check. This is called your **initiative**. The GM writes down the characters and monsters in order from highest roll to lowest. This is the order that everyone will take their turns in.

### Your Turn

On your turn, you can do two things:
*   **Move:** You can move up to your speed. For most characters, this is 30 feet.
*   **Action:** You can take one action.

You can move before or after your action.

### Actions

On your turn, you can take one **Action**. This can be anything from attacking a monster to casting a spell to trying to pick a lock.

The most common actions are:
*   **Attack:** Make a melee or ranged attack against a creature.
*   **Cast a Spell:** Cast a spell that you know.
*   **Dash:** Move up to your speed again.
*   **Use an Object:** Drink a potion, open a door, or something similar.

### Damage and Hit Points

If you hit, you roll dice to see how much damage you do. The type of weapon you are using determines the damage die. For example, a dagger might do 1d4 damage, while a greatsword does 1d12. You add your Strength modifier to the damage roll for melee attacks.

Every creature has **Hit Points (HP)**. When you take damage, you subtract it from your HP.

### Knocked Out

When your HP drops to 0, you are **knocked out**. You fall down and can't do anything. If you take any damage while you are knocked out, you die.

Another character can use their action to help you. If they do, you get 1 HP back and can act normally on your next turn.

## Experience Points

As you defeat monsters and overcome challenges, you will gain **Experience Points (XP)**. When you get enough XP, you **level up**!

When you level up, you get more powerful. You get more hit points, and you might learn new abilities. Check your class description to see what you get when you level up.

The GM will tell you when you get XP. You get XP for defeating monsters, and the GM can also give you XP for good ideas, brave actions, or for making the game fun for everyone.

## Gamemastering

Being the Gamemaster (GM) is a lot of fun. You get to be the storyteller and play all the other characters in the world. Here are a few tips to help you out.

*   **Be a fan of the characters.** Your job isn't to beat the players, but to create a fun and exciting story for them. Cheer for their successes and make their failures interesting.
*   **Be fair.** The rules are there to help you, but you can change them if it makes the game more fun. Just be consistent and fair to everyone.
*   **Improvise.** The players will do things you don't expect. That's okay! Make things up as you go. The most important thing is to have fun.
*   **Setting a DC.** When a player wants to do something, you have to decide how hard it is. Here's a simple guide:
    *   **Easy:** DC 10
    *   **Medium:** DC 15
    *   **Hard:** DC 20
